Our History!

The Scarbrough Hotel stands on the site where, in former days, there was a moated, mediaeval manor house.
Lavishly rebuilt in 1765 it had gardens extended to the river with superb views. As industrialisation in Leeds grew the residence was surrounded by workshops and soon became unsuitable as a residence.
In 1816 the estate was subdivided and in 18126 Henry Scarbrough took the property, which became the Kings Arms, an extensive hotel patronised by many distinguished guests.
The Hotel prospered until about 1863 when the railway viaduct and Queens Hotel were built nearby.
In the late 1890’s Fred Wood took over and renamed the venue The Scarbrough Hotel, named after Henry Scarbrough, it’s previous owner.
He started a great connection with Leeds City Varieties which he also owened. At the time the Scarbrough boasted a large concert hall where acts showing promise would then be put on at the Varieties.
